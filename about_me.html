About Me

Hey there! I’m Wai Yan Linn, your friendly neighborhood web developer.

In the world of web development, I like to see myself as a blacksmith, shaping code into awesome creations.

Story

At age 22, I made one of the biggest decisions of my life. I left my old career behind to follow my passion for programming. I didn’t have a degree in the field, but that didn’t matter to me. I was driven by my love for coding and creating things from scratch. It wasn’t easy at first, but I knew that with hard work, I could make it happen.
I started with the basics: HTML, CSS, and Bootstrap. These were the building blocks of everything I would go on to create. Once I had a solid foundation, I moved on to JavaScript a language that challenged me but also sparked my creativity. It was tough, especially since I had no prior experience with programming. That’s when I decided to take a class at Fairway Technology, and it was the right move. I began learning Java as a foundation to build my programming knowledge.
There was a moment during that class when everything clicked, and I truly enjoyed the whole concept of programming. I was so glad I chose this path. After completing the basics, I felt ready to dive deeper into JavaScript. I spent countless hours sometimes up to ten a day learning everything I could, focusing on one concept at a time and applying them to my projects.
Then I took the professional web developer class at Fairway Technology, and it was a game-changer for me. I learned so much about both frontend and backend development, and it’s been invaluable to my growth. As I’ve grown, I’ve learned that coding isn’t just about understanding the technical aspects; it’s about finding joy in the problem-solving process.
Looking ahead, I’m excited to keep expanding my skills, especially in backend development. My learning journey is far from over. I’m determined to keep pushing forward and achieve everything I want in this field. This journey has shown me that when I put in the work, I can achieve anything and I can’t wait to see where it takes me next.

Personal Interests

It's pretty clear that coding has become my main passion. It’s taken over most of my previous interests, and honestly, I’m okay with that. I used to spend a lot more time playing games and watching movies, but now I’d rather dive into a coding project or challenge myself with a tricky projects like this. Don’t get me wrong, I still enjoy playing games and watching movies , but they’ve taken a back seat to my current obsession.
And then there’s music. Music has been my constant companion, especially since I usually code on my own. I’ve always loved it, but it’s become even more important lately. I’m really into blues, jazz, and alternative rock, it’s what keeps me going during long coding sessions. There’s something about the flow of jazz or the vibe of blues that just clicks with my mind when I’m deep into problem-solving. It almost feels like the music is a part of the process, helping me stay focused and in the zone.
I’m definitely an introvert and pretty comfortable with that. I have a few close friends, but I don’t spend a lot of time with them. I prefer staying on my own and focusing on what I enjoy. It’s not that I’m bad at communicating, I just don’t like it as much. I’d rather observe things from the sidelines. Still, I do enjoy making new friends when the opportunity comes up. I love genuine connections and getting to know people, even if I mostly keep to myself. I am super talkactive person when I am with close friends.

Life Goals

My vision for life is pretty simple. I want to own a bread shop someday, not a huge company, but a small place where I can create and share something special. I love the idea of building something with my own hands and connecting with people in a meaningful way.
Before I reach that point, my main priority is to become a person my family can rely on and be proud of. I want to support them in every way I can. That’s why I’m working towards a career in programming, something I’m truly passionate about and excited to keep growing in. For now, I’m focused on becoming a professional in web development and building a solid foundation. But I have bigger plans. I want to dive deeper into programming, explore new technologies, and challenge myself to master different areas. I believe that with dedication, I can get where I want to be.
At the same time, I understand that life isn’t just about achievements. My priority is to live fully, take care of myself, and stay healthy. I want to avoid burning out or getting lost in the rush to achieve my goals. Ultimately, I believe I’ll reach my dreams, but I’m also taking the time to appreciate the journey.
